A little of my past, present, and future

I studied electrical engineering and computer science at MIT. After graduating in 2012, I went into management consulting, where I built predictive models for fast moving consumer good (FMCG) companies. After discovering consulting was not for me, I wanted to get back into tech. I joined the engineering team at HourlyNerd (now Catalant) in the winter of 2014. In the one years time that I spent with the engineering team at Hourlynerd I learned more than the multiple years I spent consulting. I was eager and ambitious. I venutred into buliding a product of my own in the real estate industry. From early 2016 to Summer of 2016 I worked with two other MIT students to build Transparent. Unfortunately, the real estate startup never caught traction with accelerators. Since the summer of 2016 I have been tinkering on other projects of mine and trying to discover what is next on this crasy ride called life.

From daily new york times crosswords to solving project euler problems, I try to challenge my mind and keep it nimble.

I also volunteer and do aspire to make a difference in this world. This upcoming year I plan to learn how to play guitar and start composing mixes of my own, perhaps even DJ a little.
